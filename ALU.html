The Core of Computation

The Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) is the computational heart of the CPU. It's the digital circuit where raw data is transformed into meaningful results through mathematical and logical operations.

Every action, from a simple click to complex rendering, is a series of tasks executed by this powerhouse.

The Control Center

The ALU's versatility is unlocked by just six control bits. Think of them as a command sequence telling the hardware precisely which operation to perform on the inputs, x and y.

Step 1: Pre-processing

The first four bits prepare the inputs before the main calculation.

  • zx (Zero x) & nx (Negate x)
  • zy (Zero y) & ny (Negate y)

Step 2: Core Function

The f bit dictates the main computation.

  • If f = 1: Perform Addition (x + y).
  • If f = 0: Perform a bitwise AND (x & y).

Step 3: Output & Flags

Finally, the no bit can negate the result. The ALU then produces the final 16-bit output and sets two status flags:

  • zr (Zero Flag): Is 1 if the output is 0.
  • ng (Negative Flag): Is 1 if the output is negative.
